---
title: "Schedule a maintenance task"
description: "Step-by-step guide to creating a maintenance record for a piece of equipment."
---

This workflow covers creating a new maintenance record and assigning a technician. Only equipment currently in operational status can be scheduled.

<Note>
  **Preventive vs. Corrective maintenance:**
  - **Preventivo (1):** Scheduled, routine maintenance performed to prevent failures. Use this when maintenance is planned in advance based on the equipment's maintenance schedule.
  - **Correctivo (2):** Reactive maintenance performed in response to an existing problem or failure. Use this when equipment has malfunctioned or requires unplanned repair.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify the equipment is operational">
    Go to `/pages/equipos.php` and confirm the equipment's **Estado** column shows `Funcionamiento`.

    Equipment with `Estado = Mantenimiento` is already under active maintenance and will not appear in the new maintenance dropdown.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ensure a technician exists">
    Go to `/pages/monitores.php` and confirm the technician who will perform the work is listed. If not, add them:

    1. Enter the technician's full name in the **Nombre de Monitor** field.
    2. Submit the form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to the maintenance page">
    Go to `/pages/mantenimientos.php`. The creation form appears above the maintenance records table.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the equipment">
    Choose the equipment from the **Equipo** dropdown. This dropdown only lists equipment where `estado = 1`:

    ```sql
    SELECT * FROM equipos e WHERE e.estado = 1
    ```

    If the expected equipment is missing, check its status on the equipment page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the maintenance type">
    Choose `Preventivo` or `Correctivo` from the **Tipo de Mantenimiento** dropdown based on the reason for the maintenance.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the start date">
    Enter the **Fecha de Inicio** — the date the maintenance work begins.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Describe the problem">
    Fill in the **Problema** field with a clear description of the issue or the reason for the maintenance visit. For preventive maintenance, a short note such as `Mantenimiento preventivo programado` is sufficient.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign a technician">
    Select the responsible technician from the **Monitor** dropdown.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the form">
    Click submit. Two things happen automatically:

    1. The maintenance record is inserted into the `mantenimientos` table.
    2. The equipment's status is set to `0` (Mantenimiento):

    ```sql
    UPDATE equipos SET estado = 0 WHERE id = '$idequipo'
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify the record">
    Confirm the new record appears in the maintenance table. The **Fecha Fin** column should be empty, indicating the maintenance is still open.

    On the equipment page, the equipment's **Estado** should now show `Mantenimiento`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Closing the maintenance record

Once the work is complete, the record must be closed to return the equipment to operational status.
